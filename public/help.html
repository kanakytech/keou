Keou Studio — Help & Docs

Everything you need to turn a product photo into professional visuals: get set up in two minutes, learn how generation works, and fix common issues. Free edition — you bring your own API key, you pay the AI provider directly, nothing else.

Quick start

From zero to your first generated visual in about two minutes. No account, no subscription.

1
Get a KIE.AI API key — create a free account at kie.ai, then copy your key from the API Key page. Add a few dollars of credit (a typical image costs a few cents).
2
Paste it in the Studio — open the Studio and paste your key in the bar at the top, then hit Save. The key stays in your browser only.
3
Upload & generate — drop a product photo, describe the scene you want (or pick a preset), and hit Generate. Your visual arrives in 30–90 seconds.
Best input: a clear, well-lit photo of your product on any background. The engine keeps your product pixel-locked — shape, labels, text and logos stay untouched — and builds the scene around it.

Your API key & costs

Keou Studio is bring-your-own-key: the platform is free, and generation is billed directly by the AI provider on your own account. No middleman, no markup.

Where your key lives

  • Your key is stored in your browser only (localStorage on this device).
  • It rides each generation request as a header and is never stored on our servers.
  • Clear it anytime: paste over it, or clear the site data in your browser.

What generations cost you

You buy credits directly at kie.ai and they are consumed per generation. Orders of magnitude (check current provider pricing for exact figures):

Generating images

The core workflow: one raw product photo in, a commercial-grade scene out. The product itself is preserved exactly — the engine builds lighting, environment and mood around it.

Getting great results

  • Start from a clean photo — sharp focus, decent lighting, the whole product visible. Phone photos work fine.
  • Describe the scene, not the product — the product is locked. Say where it lives: "on a marble kitchen counter, soft morning light, shallow depth of field".
  • Name a style — "editorial", "minimalist studio", "lifestyle outdoor", "luxury dark" all steer the result strongly.
  • Iterate — small prompt changes give new takes. Generate several and keep the best.
  • Batch it — queue multiple generations in parallel and let them run; progress updates live.

Aspect ratios

Pick the ratio for the destination before generating: square for feeds, portrait 9:16 for stories and reels, landscape 16:9 for banners and web headers.

Generating videos

Turn a still (uploaded or generated) into a cinematic product clip. You choose the engine per generation — each has its own strengths and price point on your provider account.

Prompting motion

  • Describe camera movement ("slow push-in", "orbit around the bottle", "handheld drift").
  • Describe scene life ("steam rising", "fabric moving in a light breeze", "light flares passing").
  • Keep it to one or two motions per clip — simple reads better and fails less.
Workflow that works: generate the perfect still first, then animate that exact image. You control the look before paying for motion.

Projects & campaigns

  • Projects group everything for one client or product line.
  • Campaigns sit inside a project — one per launch, season or channel.
  • Every generation lands in the campaign you are working in, so hand-off and retrieval stay clean.

Providers

Keou is built provider-agnostic. What that means in each edition today:

Hosted studio (this site)

One key: KIE.AI. It routes every engine above (images and all four video engines) through a single credit balance.

Self-hosted

KIE.AI and Fal.ai are both supported, with a default-provider setting and automatic fallback to whichever has a key.

Why one key on the hosted studio?

KIE.AI acts as an aggregator: a single account and credit balance covers every model the studio exposes. That keeps the free edition simple — one key to paste, one bill to watch. Broader per-user provider choice is on the roadmap; self-hosting gives you provider control today.

Self-hosting

The studio is open source. Run your own instance for durable storage, your own domain, and provider control.

1
Clone the repo — github.com/kanakytech/keou (MIT licence).
2
Configure — copy .env.example to .env: a PostgreSQL URL and a JWT secret are required; Cloudflare R2 keys are recommended for durable storage (free tier works).
3
Run — npm install && npm start, or deploy the included Dockerfile to Railway, Fly.io or any Node 20+ host.

Full instructions, environment reference and deployment notes live in the README. Issues and contributions are welcome on GitHub.

FAQ & troubleshooting

My generation failed — what do I check first?
Three usual causes, in order: 1) your provider credit balance is empty — top up at kie.ai; 2) the key was pasted with a space or is invalid — re-paste and Save; 3) the provider rejected the content (some engines refuse faces, brands or sensitive material) — adjust the image or prompt and retry.
Is my API key safe here?
The key is kept in your browser's localStorage and sent only with your own generation requests. It is never written to our database or logs. For maximum control, self-host the studio — then nothing ever leaves your infrastructure.
Why does my generation take several minutes?
Images usually land in 30–90 seconds. Video is heavier: 2–6 minutes is normal depending on the engine and provider load. The studio polls automatically — you can queue other generations meanwhile.
My product came back deformed or with wrong text
Start from a sharper, more frontal photo — product fidelity depends heavily on input quality. Avoid photos where the product is partially cut off or heavily angled. If a label is critical, mention it: "keep the label perfectly legible".
Where did my old results go?
On the free hosted studio, results rely on provider-hosted files which can expire after roughly 14 days — download what you want to keep. Self-hosted installs with R2 storage configured keep everything durably.
Can I use the visuals commercially?
The platform places no restriction of its own — usage rights for generated content follow your AI provider's terms (KIE.AI and the underlying model licences). For client work, check the current terms of the engine you used.
How do I report a bug or ask for a feature?
Open an issue on GitHub — it is read. Feedback genuinely shapes what gets built next.
